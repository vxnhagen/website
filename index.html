About houseplants

Houseplants are plants that are adapted for growing indoors, often in portable containers or miniature gardens. They are affordable and come in a variety of forms with many benefits such as purifying the air and reduce stress levels. Otherwise, houseplants improve the overall appearance of a space and are easy to raise for beginners. Perfect for people who live in busy cities where there is a lack of green spaces, a houseplant can provide a tangible connection to nature within our homes.

Strelitzia Reginae, Bird Of Paradise
Introducing the Strelitzia Reginae, commonly known as the Bird of Paradise, a botanical masterpiece that brings a touch of the tropics into your living space. With its striking resemblance to an avian paradise, this plant stands out as a regal statement piece, captivating all who encounter its majestic presence.

Benefits

As mentioned before, houseplants can purify air and you may be thinking that there is no air pollution inside your home however you may be shocked to discover that indoor air pollution exists, and this is created by aerosol sprays and cleaning products which contain volatile organic compounds that can react with the ozone in the air to form the potentially dangerous chemical formaldehyde. Another chemical that can cause problems in high levels is carbon dioxide, while ventilating areas would be of help, when it is not possible, houseplants are a good and natural way to purify the air. This solution was discovered by NASA and from that point on, waves of research into plants' potential to absorb chemicals were conducted. In addition to purifying the air and making you feel physically better, houseplants can be good for your mind too. Studies have shown that houseplants can improve our wellbeing, leading to a boost in mood, memory and productivity. Houseplants reduce stress and anxiety by just having one nearby, in a study on the effects of plants in the workplace on the level of pyschological and physiological stress of workers carried out by a Japanese team showed results that while it has no effect on physiological stress, it slightly reduces pyschological stress. Houseplants can also help with recovery after surgery, according to a study in a hospital in Korea, where patients were randomly assigned to rooms with houseplants or to a room without houseplants and the results were that patients who were hospitalized in rooms with houseplants had shorter hospital stays, took fewer painkillers, experienced less pain, anxiety, and fatigue, and had more greater satisfaction and positive emotions with their room than patients who recovered in rooms without houseplants. Furthermore, houseplants can improve attention and concentration according to a study where 23 students aged 11-13 participated in, the results were being better able to concentrate and more attentive when in the presence of a real plant than those who were not in the presence of a real plant, also a better mood in general was associated with the presence of a real plant. In enclosed spaces, houseplants can bring relief and feelings of escape, and in a case study conducted during the pandemic, significantly fewer symptoms of depression and anxiety were experienced by participants who had houseplants than those who did not. Feelings of "being away" from social or physical demands came from being surrounded by houseplants.
